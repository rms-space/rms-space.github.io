Satellite Systems

Satellites are spacecraft placed in orbit around Earth or other celestial bodies to perform communication, navigation, observation and scientific missions.

Types of Orbits

  • Low Earth Orbit (LEO)
  • Medium Earth Orbit (MEO)
  • Geostationary Orbit (GEO)

Orbital Mechanics

Orbital motion occurs when a spacecraft moves with sufficient velocity perpendicular to Earth's gravitational pull. This balance between gravity and velocity keeps satellites in orbit.

Satellite Applications

  • Communication
  • Earth Observation
  • Navigation (GPS)
  • Scientific Research
