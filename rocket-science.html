Rocket Science

Rocket propulsion is based on Newton's Third Law of Motion: for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Rocket Thrust Equation

\[ F = \dot{m}V_e + (P_e - P_a)A_e \]

Where:

  • F = Thrust
  • \(\dot{m}\) = mass flow rate
  • \(V_e\) = exhaust velocity
  • \(P_e\) = exit pressure
  • \(P_a\) = ambient pressure
  • \(A_e\) = nozzle exit area

Rocket Equation (Tsiolkovsky Equation)

\[ \Delta v = V_e \ln\left(\frac{m_0}{m_f}\right) \]

This equation describes the maximum velocity change a rocket can achieve based on exhaust velocity and propellant mass fraction.
