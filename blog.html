Understanding Rocket Propulsion

Rocket propulsion works based on Newton's Third Law of Motion: for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction. A rocket engine expels high-velocity exhaust gases through a nozzle to generate thrust, allowing the vehicle to accelerate and overcome gravitational forces.

Satellite Orbits Explained

Satellites operate in different types of Earth orbits depending on their mission. Low Earth Orbit (LEO) is commonly used for Earth observation and imaging satellites, while Geostationary Orbit (GEO) enables communication satellites to remain fixed relative to the Earth's surface.

Missile Guidance Systems

Modern missile systems rely on advanced guidance technologies such as inertial navigation systems (INS), GPS guidance and radar homing. These systems enable missiles to accurately track and reach their intended targets.
